Programmable Atomic Tweezer Array

Flexible Platform

In this project, the Will Group will demonstrate that quantum coherence can be boosted beyond the limitations of individual quantum systems. Arrays of individual neutral atoms in optical tweezers have been demonstrated as an excellent platform to study fundamental research as well as technologies involving quantum simulators, memories, atomic clocks, and metrology. An interesting, less understood dynamic found in such systems is collective radiative dissipation. These systems have potential to significantly enhance their own coherence using physics inaccessible to single atom systems. Collective radiation phenomena in atomic systems have not been well explored leaving much room for innovation and new physics to be discovered. As such, now is a ripe time to develop a novel experimental setup that draws upon current atomic array manipulation techniques with an eye for future adaptations and applications. The proposed apparatus will allow the positioning of ultracold strontium atoms at interatomic spacings to be as low as 250 nm, far below the transition wavelength 689 nm of interest, and be a flexible platform that will enable broad explorations of novel phenomena in quantum simulation, quantum computation, and quantum optics.

About Me

I am an experimental physicist pursuing my PhD at Columbia University in the City of New York. Currently, I am pursuing a novel quantum computing platform using neutral Strontium in an atomic array. I remain interested in both academic as well as private quantum computing pursuits. In addition to my research, I am a strong advocate for improving STEM education and diversity in physics.
